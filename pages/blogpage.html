A Beginner's Guide to Telescopes

By Mayank Goel | December 27, 2024

Observing the night sky is a mesmerizing experience, offering a profound connection to the vastness of the cosmos. Telescopes, first invented by Hans Lipperhey and popularized by Galileo, have served as our gateway to the universe. They played a pivotal role in laying the foundation for the Scientific Revolution, and much of the theory-observation-conclusion framework underpining the modern science can be accredited to them.

This blog as a guide will walk you through the fundamentals of telescopes, from understanding their types to observing tips and techniques, infact towards the end we will talk a bit about telescope handling.

What is a Telescope?

A telescope is an optical instrument designed to collect and focus light resulting in a magnified image formation for the distant object. A simple example of a telescope is a binocular (type of a refractive telescope). It clearly statisfies the above definition. In case of a binocular two convex lenses are used to focus the incoming light, using the binocular example we can learn a lot about refractive telescopes. Reflective telescopes which often use a parabolic mirror will be talked about later.
